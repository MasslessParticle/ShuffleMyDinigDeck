Shuffle My Dining Deck

Do you have a Denver Dining Deck? If not, go check them out.

Among other things, I love great food and Denver has no shortage of it. Having recently relocated here, I was truly excited to try the local fare.

I had a couple of problems, though. First: I didn't know anywhere to eat. Second: As I started finding places, I couldn't decide where to go next.

Enter the Dining Deck. It's a product sold by EatDenver, an association of locally-owned Independent restaurants, that has 52 $10 coupons for some of the tastiest restaurants in the Denver Metro Area. After picking up a Dining Deck for myself, I had another problem: Analysis Paralysis. With 52 choices staring at me every time I got hungry, I just couldn't decide.

Being a programmer, I decided that I'd solve this issue with code. Not only could I create something to help me know where to eat but I'd also get to play with some new technology.

This site is the result. It will choose three restaurants randomly from the Dining Deck and present you with their description, address, relative price, and phone number. You can even filter the results by the maximum cost and neighborhood. If you register, you can keep track of where you've eaten so you don't ever get stale recommendations.

This application is Open Source Software. You can find the source code at GitHub
